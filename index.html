About Me

Asok Kumar P M

Asok Kumar Pulikkaparambil Madhavan (born 17 February 1966), also known by the nickname Asokettan, is an former Indian professional footballer who captained Tamilnadu & Railway Football team in Santhosh trophy. He played as a striker, where he formed a successful attacking style and mesmerizing captaincy for his team in early 2000s.


Asok Kumar started his career with the Tamil nadu football team as Captian and rose to become one of the top names in Santhosh trophy. A highly aggressive player, he eventually became the highest scoring player in the league as well as a regular choice in the santhosh trophy and similar leagues.


Asok's talents attracted interest from the Tamilnadu, Karnataka and Railway Football team, although he spent his entire career in State Bank of India until retirement. By the end of his career he had scored 31 goals from 46 matches for his team. Since retiring from football, Asok Kumar has set upped several football coching camps academy to train young players both in his home town & other districts for free. And almost trained 5500+ students in total.


Asok Kumar has also worked as permenant employee in State Bank of India for 37 years, and received many promotions & achievement during the journey. He also associated and served for State Bank of India team during this entire period.
